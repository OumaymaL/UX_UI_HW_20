Recycle App

School group project

Research

Our research was conducted on 50% women audience and 50% male audience between the ages of 20 and 30 YO as well as 31 and 40

USER PERSONA

Jason is a progeammer based in Washington, DC. He is single and has no kids.

He is interested in implementing zero waste practices, but it feels over complicated to just get started.

He doesn’t see the big picture or overall impact, so it’s hard to feel the value of making daily practices a priority.

He knows he has more to learn, and needs to simplify making good decisions about his waste.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

“ How might we make information about recycling more accessible to motivate users to reduce their negative impact on the environment? ”

Ideation

USER SCENARIO
Goals
  • Live a cleaner lifestyle
  • Learn how to recycle various items
  • Check nearby recycling locations
Risks and emotions
  • Interest in making a positive impact on the environment
  • Frustrated by his lack of personal accountability when it comes to recycling
  • Confused by not knowing information on how to recycle specialty items
TOP TWO TAKEAWAY FEATURES
  • Map Feature with nearest recycling locations
  • Get reward points for participating in recycling
SOLUTION
Sort feature

How can we help users understand how to recycle?

  • An easy way to learn more about recycling different items
  • A customizable filter to find closest recycling locations
Map feature

How can we help users understand where to recycle?

  • Pins recycling locations based on user zip code, key word search, or filters
Calendar feature

How can we help users manage their recycling?

  • Allows to schedule pick-ups
  • Access trash/recycling schedule, based on the user address provided during sign-up
